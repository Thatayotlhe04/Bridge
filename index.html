The architecture

One memory layer. Every tool.

Bridge sits between your engineers and their AI tools — capturing, indexing, and replaying context bidirectionally. No vendor lock-in. No replatforming.

The problem

Every tool switch is a context reset.

Modern engineers don't pick one AI assistant — they use four. Each one starts every conversation from zero. Architectural decisions, codebase nuance, prior debugging context — gone. Multiple context-switching cycles waste 5-10 hours per engineer per week.

Our engineers were copy-pasting the same context paragraphs into three different tools, every single day.
— Staff Engineer, Series C fintech

Fragmented institutional knowledge

What your senior engineer figured out yesterday is trapped inside one tool's session history. Nobody else benefits.

Lost productivity at every handoff

Engineers spend the first 10–15 minutes of every tool switch re-explaining context the AI should already know.

Vendor lock-in by accident

Your team avoids the right tool because switching costs context. Bridge makes the choice neutral again.

How it works

Three steps to a unified AI workspace.

Bridge runs as a lightweight layer between your engineers and their AI tools. No replatforming. No vendor switching. Install once and conversation history flows everywhere.

01 / INSTALL

Connect your tools

Single CLI install per developer machine. OAuth into Claude Code, Codex, Cursor, and every other tool your team uses. No new accounts to provision.

02 / SYNC

History flows automatically

Every conversation, code change, and decision is captured and indexed in real time. Cross-tool sync happens in the background — invisible to the engineer.

03 / RECALL

Context follows the engineer

Open any AI tool. Bridge injects relevant history, project context, and team conventions at the start of every session. No copy-paste. No re-explaining.
